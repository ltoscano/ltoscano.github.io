  • [Neural Architectures Comparison: Common Cognitive Patterns between Brain and Artificial Intelligence] This study investigates functional analogies between hierarchical reference systems in the human neocortex and those found in modern Large Language Models, proposing that artificial neural networks can develop emergent capabilities analogous to specialized cortical areas, and explores universal computational principles underlying both biological and artificial intelligence.
    https://zenodo.org/records/15530677

  • [From Tokens to Thoughts: How LLMs and Humans Trade Compression for Meaning] This paper introduces an information-theoretic framework to compare how large language models (LLMs) and humans balance information compression with semantic fidelity in conceptual representations, revealing key differences in their approaches to meaning and categorization.
    https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.17117
