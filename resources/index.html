  • This paper investigates how Large Language Models (LLMs) and humans differ in their internal representations of concepts, focusing on the trade-off between information compression and semantic fidelity. Using an information-theoretic framework, the authors compare LLM token embeddings with human categorization data, revealing that LLMs favor aggressive statistical compression while humans prioritize nuanced, context-rich conceptual distinctions.
